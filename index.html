🧠 Why Grounding Matters (10 minutes)

Your Nervous System 101

Click on each zone to explore how your nervous system works:

🟢 Green Zone

Calm & Safe

What it feels like: Thinking clearly, feeling connected to yourself and others, able to make good decisions

Body signs: Relaxed breathing, comfortable muscle tension, present and alert

This is your optimal state for learning and connecting!

🟡 Yellow Zone

Fight or Flight

What it feels like: Anxious, angry, restless, can't sit still, mind racing

Body signs: Fast heartbeat, shallow breathing, tense muscles, fidgety

Your body is trying to protect you by preparing for action

🔴 Red Zone

Shutdown

What it feels like: Numb, spacey, can't think or feel clearly, disconnected

Body signs: Low energy, feeling heavy, hard to move or speak

Your body is protecting you by slowing everything down

🌟 How Movement Helps

  • Gets you back to Green Zone: Movement helps regulate your nervous system naturally
  • Helps you notice: Builds awareness of what zone you're in right now
  • Gives your body a voice: Movement is your body's language for feelings
  • Creates safety from the inside out: Your body learns it can handle different states

💪 Grounding = Your Superpower

  • Not about being perfect or calm all the time
  • About learning what stability feels like in YOUR unique body
  • A skill you can use anywhere, anytime
  • Gets stronger with practice, like building a muscle

🎯 Movement Exploration Stations (40 minutes)

Choose the station that calls to you today. Trust your instincts.

🌳 Station A: Find Your Roots

When you need to feel more stable

Foundation Finding (7 min)

• Stand barefoot if possible, feel connection to floor
• Gentle swaying front/back, side/side, small circles
• Find your "home base" position where you feel most stable
• Notice what grounding actually feels like in your unique body

Root Meditation Dance (15 min)

• Imagine roots growing from your feet deep into earth
• Start with tiny movements that keep your "roots" connected
• Gradually expand movement - arms like branches swaying
• Practice moving from your core while feet stay rooted

Grounding Recovery Practice (8 min)

• Intentionally move in ways that feel ungrounded
• Notice the difference in your body
• Practice returning to root connection through stillness and breath
• End appreciating your body's ability to find stability

⚖️ Station B: Balance Experiments

When everything feels wobbly

Balance Exploration (10 min)

• Start with both feet planted, shift weight slowly
• Try tree poses with modifications (toe on ground, hand on wall)
• Focus on micro-movements that help you stay balanced
• Include "wobbly tree" practice - explore imbalance safely

Seasons of Balance (15 min)

• Spring: Light, reaching movements (new growth)
• Summer: Full, expansive movements (strong and rooted)
• Fall: Releasing movements (letting go gracefully)
• Winter: Still, sturdy movements (conserving energy)

🛡️ Station C: Your Space Bubble

When boundaries feel confusing

Personal Space Discovery (10 min)

• Stand in center, slowly expand arms until you feel edge of "your space"
• Move within this bubble, explore its dimensions
• Notice if your bubble changes with different movements
• Practice expanding and contracting your space intentionally

Boundary Strength Practice (10 min)

• Practice "no" movements (hands out, stepping back, turning away)
• Practice "yes" movements (arms open, stepping forward, reaching)
• Notice body sensations between these different movements

🌊 Station D: Energy Regulation

When you're too hyped up or shut down

If Feeling Anxious/Wired:

• Start with your current energy level (don't force calm)
• Gradually slow movements down, make them smoother
• Include flowing movements like gentle swimming motions
• Focus on exhale-focused breathing while moving

If Feeling Numb/Shut Down:

• Start gentle: finger wiggling, shoulder rolls
• Gradually increase movement size and energy
• Include reaching, stretching, gentle bouncing
• Focus on bringing energy UP through movement

✍️ Station E: Move and Write

When you process better with words

Moving Meditation Writing (20 min)

• Alternate: 2 minutes gentle movement, 2 minutes writing
• Write about sensations you notice during movement
• Use present-tense language: "Right now I notice..."
• End with appreciation letter to your body

🌿 Station F: The Seven Spices

Exploring different movement flavors for healing

Try different "spices" (movement qualities) and notice what your body needs:

1. Vibration - For releasing tension (4 min)

• Gentle trembling in hands, shoulders, or whole body
• Let natural shaking happen - your nervous system releasing stress
• Don't force it, just invite the trembling

2. Sway - For self-soothing (4 min)

• Gentle side-to-side rocking like comforting yourself
• Imagine being held by someone who loves you
• Let this movement comfort whatever needs soothing

3. Wave - For processing emotions (4 min)

• Start a wave at your head, let it flow down through your body
• Follow where it wants to go, don't direct it
• Let feelings move through like water

4. Twist - For transformation (4 min)

• Gentle squirming or spiraling movements
• Like wringing out old energy that doesn't serve you
• Transform discomfort through gentle twisting

5. Shock/Impulse - For waking up (4 min)

• Quick, sudden movements - flicks, jolts, surprising gestures
• Break through numbness or stuck feelings
• Let unexpected movements wake up your system

6. Expansion - For confidence (4 min)

• Slowly grow bigger, stretch out, claim space
• Push against invisible walls, reach in all directions
• Feel your right to take up space

7. Contraction - For protection (4 min)

• Draw inward when you need safety
• Curl up, make yourself smaller, create boundaries
• Honor the wisdom of going inward

🎭 Station G: Active Imagination Movement

When you want to let your body lead

Inner Listening (8 min)

• Find stillness, close your eyes gently
• Scan your body from head to toe
• Wait for any impulse to move - tingling, pulling, warmth
• Don't force anything, just notice what wants to move

Follow the First Movement (7 min)

• Let the smallest impulse guide you
• Start tiny - maybe just a finger, shoulder, or head turn
• Don't judge or direct, let it evolve naturally
• Trust even weird or unfamiliar movements

Dance Between Moving and Being Moved (15 min)

• Follow physical sensations as they arise (heavy/light, fast/slow)
• If images pop up (animals, elements, colors), let them inform your movement
• Notice emotions that emerge, let them move through your body
• Practice being moved rather than making yourself move
• Alternate between sensing your body and letting imagination flow
